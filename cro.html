The CRO opportunity

Moving from a 2% to a 4% conversion rate doubles your revenue from the same traffic. That's the lever CRO pulls — no extra spend required.

→Message mismatch between ad and landing page accounts for up to 40% of bounce rate on average.

FAQ

Common questions

Answers to the questions we get asked before every CRO engagement.

What is conversion rate optimisation (CRO)?+
Conversion rate optimisation (CRO) is the systematic process of increasing the percentage of website visitors who take a desired action — making a purchase, submitting a form, booking a demo, or any other goal. CRO uses data analysis, user research, and A/B testing to remove friction and improve outcomes without increasing traffic or ad spend.
How much can CRO reduce customer acquisition cost?+
The impact varies by starting baseline, but brands with conversion rates below industry average typically see CAC reductions of 30–60% from CRO work alone. Mad Hat Communications achieved a 43% CAC reduction and 127% conversion lift for Alankari.com without any increase in media budget.
What does a CRO engagement with Mad Hat Communications include?+
A CRO engagement includes a full funnel audit, heatmap and session recording analysis, user research, hypothesis development, A/B test design and execution, landing page redesign, and ongoing performance reporting. We focus on the highest-leverage parts of your funnel first.
How long does CRO take to show results?+
Initial findings from a funnel audit are typically delivered within 2 weeks. A/B tests require sufficient traffic to reach statistical significance — usually 2–6 weeks per test. Most brands see measurable conversion improvement within 60–90 days of beginning a structured CRO programme.
Does CRO work for B2B SaaS as well as e-commerce?+
Yes. CRO principles apply to any conversion goal. For B2B SaaS, we optimise demo request flows, pricing page clarity, onboarding funnels, and trial-to-paid conversion paths. The methodology is the same; the metrics and friction points differ.
